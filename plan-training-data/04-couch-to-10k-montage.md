# Couch → 10K: The Montage Plan 🏃‍♀️🔥

Status: draft — 2026-07-13

## Goal
From gasping through 60-second jogs to running 10K in 16 weeks: three runs a
week, recovery weeks sacred, injuries written out of the script.

## Plan

### Act I — "I hate this" (weeks 1–4)
- [x] 1. week 1: jog 1 min / walk 2 min × 8, three times (alarm groan included)
- [~] 2. week 2: jog 2 / walk 2 × 7 — neighbor's dog now expects you
- [ ] 3. week 3: jog 4 / walk 2 × 5 — the quit-week; run with a friend (see risks) needs: 2
- [ ] 4. week 4: 🌿 recovery — walk 30 min × 3; dramatic floor stretching
- 🎉 MILESTONE: you are a person who runs three times a week — the identity shift IS the plan

### Act II — "wait, I might not hate this" (weeks 5–10)
- [ ] 5. weeks 5–6: jog 8–12 min blocks; weekend long run 25→30 min needs: 4
- [ ] 6. week 7: first 20 min continuous 😱 needs: 5
- [ ] 7. week 8: 🌿 recovery — easy 15 min × 3
- [ ] 8. weeks 9–10: run 25→30 min; long run 40→45 min — 5K distance falls here needs: 7
- 🎉 MILESTONE: first unbroken 5K — take the sweaty selfie, this is the freeze-frame

### Act III — "the final form" (weeks 11–16)
- [ ] 9. weeks 11–13: weekday 30 min holds; long run 50→60→70 min; week 12 🌿 recovery needs: 8
- [ ] 10. week 14: long run hits 8K — new-shoe checkpoint (see risks) needs: 9
- [ ] 11. week 15: taper — everything gets EASIER; trust the taper, the taper is wisdom needs: 10
- [ ] 12. week 16: RACE DAY 🏁 — nothing new: same shoes, breakfast, playlist needs: 11
- 🎉 MILESTONE: 10K finisher — roll credits; post-credits scene: "…half marathon?"

## Edge cases & risks
⚠️ RISK: week 3 is where 60% quit — novelty gone, fitness not yet arrived; schedule those runs with a friend, accountability beats motivation.
⚠️ RISK: shin splints from skipping recovery weeks — the villain of this movie; ledges are non-negotiable.
⚠️ RISK: shoe cushioning dies ~500km — week 14's 8K in dead shoes is how injury-flashback scenes get filmed.
⚠️ RISK: talk-test failure (can't speak a sentence mid-run) means pace too hot — slow down, yes slower than that.

## Open questions
